Seattle Public works approached my team believing that if users were made aware of their real time data from their smart meters, it would lead to conservation of more water.

Our task was to design a data visualization app that was engaging using gamification.

Project: Seattle Public Works

My role:

Co-researcher, project manager, interaction designer


• Began the project with user interviews.

• Created screener for both user interviews and usability testing follow up.

• A total of twelve user interviews exploring feelings towards conservation of water, understanding of water usage, relationship with Seattle Public Utilities, etc.

• Mapped pain points of users and Seattle Public Utilities

Design:

Usability Findings:

First version had no branding and made it difficult for users to tell who the app belonged to.


The usage of the water drop was definitely a hit!


Wanted more interactivity on the slider.

Because of the research, our team decided to create two disnct components within this app. The first component was a game that would hook users into using the app and think about water conservation.


The second component of the app was a utilitarian function that allowed users to check their utilities.


Both basic features would exists as distinct units but later, within the app, hook into each other: the game requires your Seattle Public Utilities Water Works information in order to continue playing.


Outcome & Evaluation:

A distinct problem presented itself throughout the user research that users did not care about water consumption as much as our client did. Our design wanted to leverage gaming as a way to hook users into enjoying the game.


The lack of caring from users on this particular issue and our solution both came as surprises.


Important Lessons Learned:

Working with strong vision it is important to refer back to the research and interrogate ideas as much as possible given time constraints.


Paper prototyping is a lifesaver.


Never doubt the power of clickable usability tests.


In tight turn around times, interaction and visual design need to run parallel to each other and not work in a waterfall method.
